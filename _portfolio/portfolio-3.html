--- title: "3D-Printed Motor From Scratch" excerpt: "Worked with some friends to build a brushed DC motor entirely from scratch
" collection: portfolio --- I took a physics lab class with two of my friends, and for our final project, we had the freedom to make whatever we wanted. We wanted to do something with magnets, and eventually settled on 3D-printing a brushed motor. The most difficult part of this project was making the commutator, which is the piece of the motor that takes advantage of the shaft's rotation to switch the polarity of the electromagnet (and therefore the direction of the magnetic field). Our motor wasn't built to be particularly robust, it was more of a challenge to prove to ourselves that we could build a motor, but we did end up having an average RPM of around 1460 RPM, which was a lot better than we expected given the materials we were working with.
