I was in my high school robotics team for five years, during which I learned an incredible amount about robotics, teamwork, and leadership. A selection of the technichal projects I was involved with include:

- Swerve drive control and wiring
- Autonomous routines that ran at the start of every match
- Motion-profiling for use with complex arm mechanisms
- Vision targeting and aiming for shooting games
- Rapid troubleshooting (often interconnected) electrical and programming issues under pressure during competitions

I led the programming subteam and the electrical subteam starting in my freshman year, and initially, most of my work revolved around getting the robot competition-ready, programming robot-specific code, and testing autonomous routines. However, as I became a more senior member of the team, I realized that an important part of my role was to pass on what I knew to younger students so that the team could continue operating at a high level after I graduated.

Most of our code was written in Kotlin (Java in 2017-- we switched languages), though some smaller pieces such as vision were occasionally written in Python or C/C++.
